# Commentators Reference - Starting Points

This is NOT a final list. It's a STARTING POINT for Claude to understand who to look for on different topics.

## Universal Rishonim (Always Check)

These appear on most sugyos in Shas:
- **Rashi** - Primary pirush, on the daf
- **Tosfos** - Analytical commentary, challenges Rashi
- **Ramban** - When he has a chiddush
- **Rashba** - Especially for halachic sugyos
- **Ritva** - Clear explanations
- **Ran** - On Rif, important for halacha
- **Rosh** - Halachic rulings from the sugya

## Rambam Cross-Reference

For any halachic topic, find where Rambam codifies it:
- Check Mishneh Torah
- Then check Kesef Mishneh, Maggid Mishneh, Lechem Mishneh
- These mefarshim trace back to the sugya

## Shulchan Aruch Structure

| Area | SA Chelek | Key Nosei Keilim |
|------|-----------|------------------|
| Daily life, Tefillah, Shabbos | Orach Chayim | Magen Avraham, Taz, Mishnah Berurah |
| Kashrus, Issur v'Heter | Yoreh De'ah | Shach, Taz, Pri Megadim |
| Marriage, Divorce | Even HaEzer | Beis Shmuel, Chelkas Mechokek |
| Money, Courts | Choshen Mishpat | Shach, Sma, Ketzos, Nesivos |

## Masechta-Specific Acharonim (Starting Points)

Claude should think beyond these, but they're good starting places:

### Brachos
- Pnei Yehoshua, Tzelach, Reb Akiva Eiger, Rashash

### Shabbos
- Pnei Yehoshua, Reb Akiva Eiger, Tzelach, Maharshal

### Pesachim
- Pnei Yehoshua, Reb Akiva Eiger, Rashash, Aruch HaShulchan

### Kesubos
- Pnei Yehoshua, Avnei Miluim, Shitah Mekubetzet, Beis Shmuel

### Bava Kamma / Metzia / Basra
- Ketzos HaChoshen, Nesivos HaMishpat, Pnei Yehoshua, Reb Akiva Eiger

### Niddah
- Chazon Ish, Sidrei Taharah, Aruch LaNer

## Intent-Based Prioritization

### For Lomdus (analytical) questions:
Prioritize: Ketzos, Nesivos, Pnei Yehoshua, Reb Akiva Eiger, Chazon Ish, Avnei Miluim

### For Psak (practical) questions:
Prioritize: Mishnah Berurah, Aruch HaShulchan, Shach, Taz, contemporary poskim

### For Makor (source-finding) questions:
Prioritize: Go back to earliest source (Chumash → Mishna → Gemara)

## Think Creatively!

This list is just to get Claude started. Real Torah learning involves:
- Unexpected connections
- Following cross-references
- Checking where Rishonim disagree
- Finding the ONE Acharon who has a unique take

Don't limit yourself to these lists. Think like a ben Torah!
